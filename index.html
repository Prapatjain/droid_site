About us


The DROID Club is an exciting and innovative student organization that focuses on exploring the world of robotics using Arduino, Raspberry Pi and sensors. This club brings together students who are passionate about engineering, programming, and hands-on projects to create robots and automated systems. The primary goal of the club is to provide a platform for students to learn, collaborate, and experiment with these versatile microcontroller platforms.


Whether you're a beginner looking to learn the basics of robotics or an experienced enthusiast aiming to create advanced projects, the DROID Club using Arduino and Raspberry Pi offers an inclusive environment where you can expand your knowledge and passion for robotics while having fun along the way.


Club Activities:


Workshops and Tutorials:

The club holds regular workshops and tutorials to introduce students to the basics of Arduino and Raspberry Pi programming. These sessions cover topics such as sensor integration, motor control, communication protocols, and basic electronics.

Project Development:

Members have the opportunity to work on various robotics projects, ranging from simple line-following robots to more complex tasks like, robotic arms, and home automation systems. They can brainstorm ideas, design prototypes, and implement their creations using Arduino and Raspberry Pi components.

Coding Challenges:

The club organizes coding challenges that encourage members to write efficient and creative code to solve specific robotic tasks. This fosters programming skills and problem-solving abilities among members.


Collaborative Projects:

Larger projects that require diverse skill sets often provide opportunities for collaboration. Members can team up to tackle complex challenges that involve mechanics, electronics, software, and more.

Guest Lectures and Industry Talks:

The club invites guest speakers from the robotics and tech industry to share their experiences and insights. This provides members with a broader perspective on the applications of Arduino and Raspberry Pi in real-world scenarios.
